Clustering Application – K-Means with Scikit-learn
Data Context

This application applies the K-Means clustering algorithm to a dataset of 100 people collected in class, containing their age and monthly income (COP).

The goal is to segment individuals into natural groups based on these two variables, identifying patterns that could be useful for economic analysis, market segmentation, or social policy design.

Context: Income-Age Segmentation using K-Means Clustering
Dataset Explanation

Number of records: 100 people

Features used for clustering:

  • Edad (Age): Age of the person in years (range: 18–70)
  • Ingresos (Monthly Income): Monthly income in COP thousands (range: $650K – $6,500K)

No preprocessing labels or categories exist — this is raw, unlabeled data.

Data Preprocessing

Before running K-Means, the two features are normalized using StandardScaler (z-score normalization) so that Age and Income contribute equally to the distance calculation.

z = (x − μ) / σ
  • Age: mean ≈ 42.6, std ≈ 14.1
  • Income: mean ≈ 3,425, std ≈ 1,631

Without scaling, Income (in thousands) would dominate over Age in the Euclidean distance formula.

Preprocessing: StandardScaler applied to both features (Edad, Ingresos)
